Mapping Flood Risk in Affordable Housing: An Examination of LIHTC Developments from 2015-2018

The Low Income Housing Tax Credit (LIHTC) is the largest program assisting the development of affordable housing, which is desperately needed in New York City. However, NYC is also extremely vulnerable to sea level rise and the increasing severity of storms that accompanies climate change. Using publicly available elevation data from USGS, HUD CSV data on LIHTC properties, FEMAâ€™s Preliminary 2015 FIRMs and incorporating research from NYC that estimates flood risk as sea levels rise in the coming decades, I mapped recently built affordable housing developments that are at risk of flooding. Results showed as few as 13 and as many as 28 properties with high flood risks, accounting for several thousand units of housing and tens of millions of dollars in annual tax credit allocations. These results point to the necessity of incorporating climate change in all aspects of public policy, especially affordable housing.
